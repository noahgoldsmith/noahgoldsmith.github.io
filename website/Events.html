Blessup Live
The event consisted of a vendor fair, live performances, and a fashion show where participants sported the Blessup Brand. The Vendor fair allowed my brand to sell merchandise.  It also hosted a bakery, a recording artist, a natural soap company, and a custom t-shirt business. While all the shopping was going on, there was live music and refreshments for everyone to enjoy. Following the vendor fair, we kicked off the start of the show with live performances with Tamika Foggie, Recording Artist out of St. Louis Blessing the house with her angelic voice, following her we had a choir travel from Nebraska, and premiered a new music video we shot at a local church. To end the performances, we had 2 gospel rappers close the show. Of course, this being the first-time event happened, I made sure to capture plenty of audio and video recorded for the sake of promoting the next event. Most of the revenue came from merchandise sales, as regular admission was only $10. Some people chose to sponsor the event with the ability to purchase different tickets that cost more.

After the show
It was a warm summer night. As we packed up the show, the saxophone player was still jamming in the circle drive and my ex is walking through the doors with her prom date. I gave her a big hug after not seeing her for 2 years, shared a few words, dapped up her prom date and went back home. As I digressed, I looked at the media acquired from the event and saw overwhelming smiles from everybody who attended the event. It was as if they forgot about all the struggles that everyday life brings.
